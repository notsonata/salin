# Product

## Register

product

## Users
Filipino students, professionals, and small teams who need to review uploaded Tagalog, English, or Taglish recordings after the fact.

They are usually working from lectures, meetings, interviews, discussions, and client calls. Their job is not simply to obtain raw transcription output, but to verify what was said, extract usable notes, and carry that material into follow-up work without replaying the full recording repeatedly.

## Product Purpose
Salin converts uploaded recordings into a reviewable workspace with timestamped transcripts, generated notes, action items, summaries, and editable downstream outputs.

Its purpose is to make mixed-language recordings easier to study, audit, and turn into useful written artifacts. Success looks like a user opening one dashboard and being able to read the transcript, copy or reuse it, generate notes, edit those notes, and move on with confidence.

## Brand Personality
Salin should feel precise, studious, premium, and bilingual-native.

The tone should communicate competence, calm focus, and earned trust. It should feel like a serious workspace for reading and reviewing language-rich material, not a flashy automation toy.

## Anti-references
- Generic SaaS dashboard kits
- Flashy "AI copilot" visuals and gimmicks
- Pastel, over-rounded startup UI
- Any visual or verbal framing that implies live meeting assistance, perfect speaker identification, or careless automation

## Design Principles
- Transcript-first workflow: the transcript is the primary artifact, and every supporting surface should strengthen reviewability rather than compete with it.
- Honest intelligence: automation should feel useful and capable without overstating certainty, especially around speaker labels, transcription quality, and generated notes.
- Bilingual fluency without ceremony: Tagalog, English, and Taglish use cases should feel native to the product rather than bolted on as edge cases.
- Dense but calm workspace: the interface should support serious work, copy/paste, editing, and comparison without becoming noisy, ornamental, or generic.
- Notes as a working continuation: summaries, action items, and edited notes should feel like the next step of transcript review, not a detached AI side feature.

## Accessibility & Inclusion
Salin should meet WCAG AA as a baseline.

Reduced-motion support is required. Status, error, and progress states must remain understandable without relying on color alone. Keyboard reachability and clear transcript readability should be preserved across upload, review, notes, and failure states.
